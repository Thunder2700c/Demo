::: scene label="Emiya Heights — 02:13"

The kettle had boiled itself dry twice before either of them spoke again.

Haruto's apartment was small and rented and full of the particular silence of a place that had only ever held one person. Now it held two, and the second one stood by the window with the posture of someone who had been carved out of marble and then taught, very late in life, how to be polite about it. The sword had been set down on the kitchen counter, next to the half-empty jar of instant coffee, and it looked no less dangerous for the indignity.

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"You should sleep, Master. The summoning drew from your od. Your reserves are low."
:::

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"I'll sleep when you tell me your name."
:::

A pause. The Servant did not turn from the window. Outside, Fuyuki had gone quiet in the way cities do at two in the morning — not silent, but listening.

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"A True Name is a blade pointed at its owner. Every Servant who spoke theirs freely in a Grail War paid for it. You know this. Your grandmother taught you the rules."
:::

Haruto's hand tightened around his coffee mug. The three Command Spells glowed faintly on his skin, three small red figures that ached when he flexed his fingers — a dull, warm ache, like holding a coal that had decided to be patient.

He had read everything his grandmother left behind. The handwritten notebooks, the brittle photographs, the careful diagrams of seven classes arranged around a single chalice. *Saber, Archer, Lancer, Rider, Caster, Assassin, Berserker.* Seven roles. Seven hungers. And only one of them could walk away with the prize that the Grail was not, despite the name, a cup.

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"Then tell me your class. Tell me something. I dragged you into a city that wants me dead and I don't even know what to call you."
:::

The Servant turned at last. The cyan light of the streetlamp outside caught the edge of the blue armor and laid a thin bright wire along one shoulder.

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"Saber," it said. "You may call me that. It is the only title that will not cost either of us our lives tonight."
:::

A fair bargain. Haruto hated how reasonable it sounded.

He set the mug down and crossed to the window, standing a careful arm's length from his Servant — from *Saber* — and looked out over the wet rooftops. Somewhere out there, six others were waking to the same quiet, private alarm. Six Masters. Six blades in the dark. And at the end of all of it, a Grail that his grandmother had underlined three times in red ink and labelled, in her shaking final hand, *do not touch.*

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"Saber. Did you choose me? Or did the Grail?"
:::

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"There is a third answer you have not considered, Master."
:::

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"Which is?"
:::

Before the answer could come, every light in the apartment went out at once, and the air pressure in the room dropped so sharply that Haruto's ears popped. The Command Spells on his hand blazed — all three of them — and Saber moved without sound, scooping the sword from the counter in a motion so fast it seemed to edit time out of the equation.

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"Get behind me. Now."
:::

The window did not break. It *opened* — by itself, the latch turning with a polite little click — and through the gap came the smell of cold iron and wet dog and something older, something that tasted of deep water and held breath.

A voice floated up from the street below, light and almost cheerful.

::: dialogue speaker="Lancer" variant="crimson"
"There you are. I was beginning to think the War had started without me. Come down and fight, little Master — or do I have to come up and fetch you?"
:::

Haruto felt Saber's gauntleted hand close, very gently, on his shoulder.

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"The third answer, Master — is that something chose us *both*. And it is not the Grail. Stay close."
:::
