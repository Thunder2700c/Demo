::: scene label="Fuyuki Bridge — 23:47"

The rain came down in long grey knives, and Haruto counted every one of them.

He had stopped running three streets back. Now he stood at the center of the bridge with one hand pressed against his ribs, where the wound had gone past pain and into something quieter — a deep, polite cold that introduced itself to his bones and refused to leave. Below him the Mion River swallowed the storm without complaint, black and patient as an open mouth.

So this is how the Emiyas and the Tohsakas felt, he thought. Right at the end. Right before the circle lit up.

He had never wanted the War. He had only wanted to understand why his grandmother whispered a chalk pattern into the floorboards before she died, and why his right hand had burned with three red marks the morning after the funeral. The city had answered him in its own language: a knife between the ribs, an alley with no exit, and the slow understanding that the marks were not a birthright but a death sentence.

Three Command Spells. Three orders that could bend a hero to his will. And no idea how to use even one of them.

His knees hit the asphalt. The chalk in his pocket — stolen from the school art room, of all places — had gone soft in the rain, and he drew the circle anyway, half from memory and half from the burning diagram that the Marks were kindly projecting into the back of his skull. It was ugly work. The lines wobbled. The outer ring looked like a child's first attempt at the sun.

It would do. It would have to.

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"I don't know your name. I don't know your class. I don't even know if you can hear me."
:::

He pressed his bleeding palm flat against the center of the circle and spoke the words his grandmother had made him memorize in a fever, three nights before the end — words he had promised himself he would never say.

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"But if you're listening — come. Fill. Set."
:::

The rain stopped.

Not faded. *Stopped.* Every drop hung in the air like glass beads on a broken string, and the river below went mirror-still, and the whole of Fuyuki seemed to hold a single breath. The circle answered him with light — not the warm gold of the stories, but a clean, surgical cyan that climbed his arm in runnels and made the Command Spells on his hand flare like three small suns.

::: impact effect="glow" intensity="high"
Let the Seventh War be the last.
:::

There was a sound like the whole sky tearing along a seam. Wind that was not wind poured out of nowhere and pressed Haruto flat to the ground, and through the white noise he heard footsteps — one, two, three — calm and unhurried, crossing a distance that should not have been crossable.

The light folded itself up like an origami bird and put itself away.

And standing in the center of his crooked chalk circle, dry as a held secret, was a figure in blue and silver armor that had no right to exist outside of a museum or a dream.

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"I ask of you — are you my Master?"
:::

Haruto looked up through the rain that had begun, very gently, to fall again. He looked at the sword that rested in the figure's hand like a sentence waiting for a period. He looked at the eyes, which were the exact cyan of the circle's light, and which held no warmth at all and yet — somehow — held no cruelty either.

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"I think," he said carefully, "that I'm about to find out."
:::
