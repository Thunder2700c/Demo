::: scene label="Shinto District — 21:08"

It started, as these things did, with the streetlights dying one by one.

Haruto noticed it first as a feeling — a wrongness in his teeth, a pressure behind his eyes, like the moment before a nosebleed that never quite arrives. Saber felt it a half-second later and went still in that particular way that meant *do not move and do not breathe.*

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"The boundary field is folding. Something is rewriting the leyline. Master — look at the sky."
:::

Haruto looked up, and the sky was wrong.

It was the same sky. The same low clouds, the same rain, the same pale wash of Fuyuki's light pollution against the underbellies of the storm. And it was not the same sky at all, because the stars behind the clouds were in the wrong order — reversed, like a page held up to a mirror — and the moon, which had been a crescent an hour ago, was now full, and old, and looking at him.

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"Saber. That's not possible. We watched it set."
:::

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"No. It is not possible. And yet."
:::

The air took on the taste of burnt iron and something colder — ozone's quieter cousin, the smell of a place where the rules had been politely set aside. Around them, the street began to change in small, terrible ways. A shop sign he had passed a hundred times now read in a script he did not recognise. A clock in a window showed a time that had not been invented yet. And every puddle on the ground reflected a city that was almost Fuyuki but not — taller, broken in different places, wearing its wounds like a different person's face.

::: scene label="The Inverted Boundary"

And then the figure stepped out of the loop in the air.

It did not walk. It *resolved*, the way a photograph resolves in developing fluid — first a smudge, then a shape, then a person standing in the middle of the road as if they had always been there and the road had simply failed to notice. They wore no armour. They carried no visible weapon. They were dressed in a coat the colour of old paper, and they looked at Haruto with an expression he could not name, because it was the expression of someone who had been waiting a very long time and had almost given up.

::: dialogue speaker="???" variant="crimson"
"There you are. I have crossed nine versions of this city to stand here, and every one of them ended the same way — with you dead on a bridge, with your hand on a chalk circle, asking a Saber if it could hear you."
:::

Haruto's blood went cold, and then colder, and then turned to something that was not blood at all but memory — pure, drowning memory — and he knew, without being told, that the figure was telling the truth.

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"I've never... I've never died on that bridge."
:::

::: dialogue speaker="???" variant="crimson"
"Not in this loop. Not yet. Listen to me carefully, little Master, because the field will fold back in ninety seconds and I will not remember standing here. The Grail is not a cup. It is a key. And the door it opens has been opening, over and over, every War, for longer than your grandmother knew. You are not the seventh. You are the *last.*"
:::

::: impact effect="shatter" intensity="high"
THE LOOP MUST CLOSE.
:::

The world jolted. The streetlights came back, all at once, buzzing and offended, and the figure in the paper coat was simply gone — not vanished, but *absent*, the way a word is absent from a sentence when you remove it and the grammar quietly heals around the gap.

Saber's sword was already half-drawn, pointed at the empty air where the stranger had stood.

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"Master. What did it mean? What is the *last*?"
:::

Haruto stared at the wet asphalt. His Command Spells — both remaining marks — had gone dark and cold on his hand, as if they, too, were trying to remember something they had never been told.

He thought of his grandmother's red ink. *Do not touch the Grail.* He thought of the bridge. He thought of the chalk circle, and the cyan light, and the single held breath of a city that had done this all before.

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"I think, Saber... it means this isn't the first time we've had this conversation. And I think it's supposed to be the last."
:::

The rain kept falling. The moon — crescent again, ordinary again — climbed back behind the clouds. And somewhere deep beneath Fuyuki, in a place that had no name and no floor, something very old turned a key it had been turning for centuries, and waited, with the patience of a river, to see whether this loop would finally, finally break.
