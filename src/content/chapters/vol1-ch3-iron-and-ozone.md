::: scene label="Fuyuki Rooftops — 02:31"

They took the fight to the sky because Saber refused to let it touch the ground.

Haruto had not known a person could *run* across power lines. Saber did it the way other people crossed a room — three quick strides up the side of the building, a leap that left the rain hanging, and then they were two stories up and rising, with Haruto's wrist locked in a grip that did not bruise and did not let go and did not, he noted, even breathe hard.

Lancer followed them like a rumour.

The spearman moved the way fire does: without weight, without warning, in long orange arcs that carved the air apart and left the smell of burning ozone behind. Every time Saber parried, the rooftop under their feet lost another chunk, and Haruto learned very quickly that a battle between Heroic Spirits was less a duel and more a slow, polite demolition of the surrounding architecture.

::: dialogue speaker="Lancer" variant="crimson"
"Not bad, Saber! Not bad at all. Hold still and let me get a look at your face — I want to know whose legend I'm interrupting."
:::

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"You'll have to earn that, Lancer."
:::

The next exchange was too fast for Haruto to read. He saw only the afterimage — two streaks of light crossing, a shower of sparks that hung in the rain like a constellation falling sideways, and then Lancer skidding back ten meters with a laugh that was genuinely delighted.

::: dialogue speaker="Lancer" variant="crimson"
"There it is. The real thing. You've made my whole night, you know that?"
:::

Haruto's heart was hammering so hard he could feel it in the Command Spells. Three marks. Three orders. His grandmother's handwriting floated up behind his eyes: *A Command Spell is not a request. It rewrites reality for a single instant. Use them only when reality is wrong.*

Reality, right now, was a spearman who had stopped playing.

Lancer planted his feet and the air around the spearhead began to gather itself — a slow, heavy intake, like the city taking a breath to scream. Haruto felt Saber tense, felt the whole contract between them pull taut as a wire, and understood with a cold clear certainty that the next second was going to cost one of them everything.

::: dialogue speaker="Lancer" variant="crimson"
"You've been polite long enough. Let me show you mine —"
:::

::: impact effect="shake" intensity="high"
STOP.
:::

Haruto did not know he was going to speak. The word tore itself out of his throat in a voice that did not sound entirely like his own, and the first Command Spell on his hand blew apart in a flare of red light that wrapped around Lancer like a fist made of pure grammar — *stop* — and the spearman's charging Noble Phantasm stuttered, seized, and held.

One second. The Spell bought him one second.

It was enough.

Saber moved in that borrowed instant like a line being drawn between two points — the shortest, most brutal distance — and the flat of the great sword took Lancer across the ribs and sent him cartwheeling off the edge of the roof and down into the dark, where he landed with a crash and a final, startled bark of laughter that echoed up the rain.

::: dialogue speaker="Lancer" variant="crimson"
"A Command Spell — on the very first night! Oh, Master of Saber, you are *fun.* We'll do this again."
:::

And then he was gone, swallowed by the storm, and the rooftop was quiet except for the rain and the slow drip of melting asphalt.

Haruto looked down at his hand. Two marks left. The first had been a price, and he had paid it without thinking, and the empty place where it had been throbbed like a missing tooth.

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"You saved my life, Master."
:::

::: dialogue speaker="Haruto" variant="cyan"
"I wasted a third of our power in the first hour. On a fight we could have run from."
:::

Saber regarded him with those depthless cyan eyes, and for the first time something moved in them that was not calculation.

::: dialogue speaker="Saber" variant="cyan"
"You did not run. That is the lesson your grandmother could not teach you from a notebook. Come. We are exposed here, and the rain will bring others."
:::

Haruto followed. Behind them, the cut rooftop hissed and cooled, and the ozone smell faded, and somewhere far below the Mion River went on swallowing the storm without complaint, the way it always had, the way it always would — for as long as there was a Fuyuki to swallow it.
